Emergency

Negligence in dealing with emergency situations in which your pet is exposed, such as fractures or burns, can lead to serious consequences, including death.

You must deal with these situations quickly and carefully, and it is recommended to communicate with a specialized veterinarian to deal properly.

A foreign body in your cat's throat
If you fear your cat has a foreign object trapped in the throat, visit a veterinarian right away. Depending on the size and location of the object, an esophageal or pharyngeal blockage can be a life-threatening condition Try to pull it out with tongs. If you cannot remove the stuck object with the forceps, Put your cat on your lap and try to get the object out by pressing on it
What do I do when my turtle gets burned by hot water?
Early stages: Redness and inflammation of the skin The appearance of water bubbles under the skin It is injured from the bottom of the shell and becomes soft and red in the back Late stages: Abstaining from food for long periods The tail becomes black, the legs become black and soft falling nails Quickly raise it from the water and put it in water of an appropriate temperature * not cold * free of chlorine ; Putting (povidone-iodine) or betadine on the body parts away from the eyes Only wash her head with chlorine-free water Put it in a dry bowl for 30 minutes, then wash all parts of its body except the eyes with chlorine-free water Put it in a bowl, add and dry
Thermal burns to my dog
In the event of a thermal burn involving steam, boiling water, or fire, you can try placing 3 tea bags in a cup of hot water and then adding plenty of ice to cool it down and applying it with a cotton swab. • Before you take your dog to the vet, put a splint on the broken limb. If your dog sprains his wrist or ankle, apply a cold compress and take him to the vet. • Take a piece of cardboard or something hard and flat such as a pacifier stick and secure it to the cat's end with a bandage. You can stop minor bleeding by putting some gauze on the wound and something cold on top. In the case of profuse bleeding, make a tourniquet by wrapping a bandage tightly around the area above the wound.

If you have a pet and are having difficulty assessing and understanding its behaviour, we can help you with that, in:
